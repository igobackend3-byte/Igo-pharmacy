Ayurveda Basics

Vata, Pitta, Kapha — What Your Dosha Means and How to Shop for It

The three doshas are Ayurveda's way of describing your body's working style. Vata governs movement — when aggravated it shows up as joint stiffness, dryness and restlessness, and is soothed by warm oils like Dhanwantharam or Sahacharadi Thailam. Pitta governs transformation — excess brings acidity, skin flare-ups and irritability, calmed by cooling kwaths and ghrithams. Kapha governs structure — imbalance appears as congestion, sluggish metabolism and weight gain, lifted by stimulating rasayanams and light kwaths. Most people are a blend; the right routine targets whichever is currently aggravated.

Classical Formulations

Kwath, Arishtam, Choornam, Thailam — A Buyer's Guide to Dosage Forms

A kwath is a concentrated herbal decoction — fast-acting, taken diluted before food; many are also available as convenient tablets. An arishtam or asavam is naturally fermented, self-preserving and gentle for longer courses. A choornam is a fine herb powder — flexible dosing, best fresh. A thailam or keram is a medicated oil for massage, scalp or skin. A lehyam is an herbal jam (like Chyavanprash) that combines nutrition with medicine, and a ghritham is medicated ghee that carries herbs deep into tissues. Choose the form that fits your routine — the classical recipe inside is often the same.

Everyday Wellness

Building a Simple Daily Ayurveda Routine That Actually Sticks

You don't need ten products. Start with one goal: digestion (Triphala at night), immunity (a spoon of Chyavanprash each morning), sleep and stress (Ksheerabala oil foot massage before bed), or joints (a weekly warm-oil abhyanga with Mahanarayana Thailam). Give any routine 4–6 weeks — Ayurveda works with your body's rhythm, not against it. Pair it with warm water, regular meals and early dinners, and consult a physician before combining herbs with ongoing medication, in pregnancy, or for chronic conditions.
