Before You Start

Typical documentation for exporting agri-produce from India.

  • IEC (Import Export Code) — issued by DGFT, mandatory for any export/import transaction under your business name.
  • APEDA registration — required if your product falls under APEDA's scheduled list (most processed foods, fruits, vegetables, cereals).
  • Phytosanitary certificate — issued by the Plant Quarantine department, confirming the produce is pest/disease-free for the destination country.
  • Quality/grading certification — varies by buyer and destination country; some markets also require FSSAI or organic certification.
  • Minimum order quantity — set by the buyer/agency, not by us; typically container-load quantities for export, so this is worth confirming early with whichever licensed agency you work with.

This is general awareness, not a complete or binding checklist — requirements vary by product and destination. Confirm exact documentation with a licensed export/import agency (see Step 3) before committing.
